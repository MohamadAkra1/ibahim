Places to Visit

The most beautiful and historic landmarks of Hama

Top Landmarks

From ancient ruins to serene gardens

The Norias

Hama is famous for its 17 ancient water wheels along the Orontes River, some dating back to the 11th century, used to lift water into aqueducts for irrigation.

Historic Buildings

The old city features beautiful traditional buildings with stunning courtyards, stone masonry, and intricate architectural details from the Ottoman era.

The Orontes River

The river flows through the heart of Hama, creating beautiful parks and gardens along its banks. Perfect for evening walks.

The Great Mosque

Also known as the Umayyad Mosque, built in the 12th century with stunning Islamic architecture and elegant minarets.

Al-Azm Palace

Built in the 18th century, a masterpiece of traditional Syrian architecture now housing the National Museum of Hama.

River Parks

The banks of the Orontes are beautifully landscaped with parks and gardens, especially beautiful when the norias are illuminated.
