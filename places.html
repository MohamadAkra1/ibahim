Places to Visit

Discover the most beautiful and historic landmarks of Hama

Top Landmarks

From ancient ruins to serene gardens, Hama offers a wealth of attractions

The Norias (Water Wheels)

Hama is famous for its 17 ancient norias (water wheels) along the Orontes River. These engineering marvels, some dating back to the 11th century, are used to lift water into aqueducts for irrigation. The largest, known as the "Norias of Hama," are a symbol of the city.

The Citadel of Hama

The ancient citadel sits on a large mound in the center of the city, with evidence of habitation dating back to the 3rd millennium BC. It offers panoramic views of Hama and has been rebuilt many times throughout its history.

The Great Mosque of Hama

Also known as the Umayyad Mosque, this beautiful mosque was originally built in the 12th century. It features stunning Islamic architecture with elegant minarets, courtyards, and intricate decorations.

Al-Azm Palace

Built in the 18th century by the Ottoman governor As'ad Pasha al-Azm, this palace is a masterpiece of traditional Syrian architecture. It now houses the National Museum of Hama with artifacts from the region's rich history.

The Orontes River Gardens

The banks of the Orontes River are beautifully landscaped with parks and gardens. Locals and visitors enjoy walking along the river, especially in the evening when the norias are illuminated.

The Old Souk

Hama's traditional market (souk) is a maze of narrow covered streets filled with shops selling spices, textiles, handicrafts, and local products. It's a wonderful place to experience the traditional atmosphere of an Arab market.
