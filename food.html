Local Food

Taste the traditional flavors of Hama

Famous Dishes

Hama's cuisine reflects the rich agricultural heritage of the region

Kibbeh

A signature dish of the region, kibbeh is made from ground meat mixed with bulgur wheat and spices. In Hama, it is often prepared in the traditional style as "Kibbeh Labanieh" (with yogurt) or fried as crispy balls.

Fattet Hummus

A delicious layered dish made with crispy bread, chickpeas, yogurt, and tahini. This is one of the most popular dishes in Hama, often served at special occasions and family gatherings.

Maqluba

Meaning "upside down" in Arabic, this famous dish features layers of rice, vegetables, and meat, all cooked together and then flipped to create a beautiful presentation. It's a staple at family gatherings.

Shawarma

While shawarma is popular throughout the Middle East, Hama's version is particularly renowned. Thinly sliced meat from a rotating spit is served in warm bread with garlic sauce, pickles, and fresh vegetables.

Syrian Sweets

Hama is famous for its traditional sweets, especially baklava, maamoul (date-filled cookies), and halawet el-jibn (sweet cheese dessert). These treats are an essential part of celebrations and hospitality.

Hama Cherries

Hama is renowned throughout Syria for its delicious cherries. The cherry orchards in the surrounding areas produce some of the finest cherries in the region. The annual Cherry Festival celebrates this local treasure.
