Interesting Facts

Surprising facts that make Hama unique

Did You Know?

Fascinating facts about this remarkable city

1

One of the Oldest Cities in the World

Continuously inhabited for over 8,000 years, with evidence of settlement dating back to around 6000 BC.

2

The City of Water Wheels

Called "The City of Water Wheels" because of its 17 ancient norias, some up to 20 meters in diameter.

3

The Orontes River

One of the few rivers in the Middle East that flows northward, the lifeblood of Hama for thousands of years.

4

Famous Cherries

Renowned throughout Syria for its delicious cherries, celebrated every year at the Cherry Festival.

5

Ancient Soap-Making Tradition

Centuries-old tradition of making olive oil soap using traditional methods still prized for its quality.

6

Fourth Largest City in Syria

Population of over 800,000 people, the capital of Hama Governorate and an important economic center.

7

Strategic Location

At the crossroads of major trade routes connecting Damascus to Aleppo throughout history.

8

Home to Many Scholars

Home to the historian Ibn al-Athir and near the birthplace of poet Abu al-Ala al-Ma'arri.
