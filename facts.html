Interesting Facts

Discover surprising and fascinating facts about Hama

Did You Know?

Fascinating facts that make Hama truly unique

1

One of the Oldest Cities in the World

Hama has been continuously inhabited for over 8,000 years, making it one of the oldest cities in the world. Evidence of settlement dates back to around 6000 BC.

2

The City of Water Wheels

Hama is called "The City of Water Wheels" because of its 17 ancient norias. These massive wooden wheels, some up to 20 meters in diameter, have been lifting water from the Orontes River for centuries.

3

The Orontes River

The Orontes River is one of the few rivers in the Middle East that flows northward. It runs through the heart of Hama and has been the lifeblood of the city for thousands of years.

4

Famous Cherries

Hama is renowned throughout Syria for its delicious cherries. The surrounding countryside produces some of the finest cherries in the region, celebrated every year at the Cherry Festival.

5

Ancient Soap-Making Tradition

Hama has a centuries-old tradition of making olive oil soap, known as "sabun ghar." This natural soap is still produced today using traditional methods and is prized for its quality.

6

Fourth Largest City in Syria

Hama is the fourth largest city in Syria, with a population of over 800,000 people. It is the capital of Hama Governorate and an important economic and cultural center.

7

Strategic Location

Hama is located at the crossroads of major trade routes connecting Damascus to Aleppo. This strategic position has made it an important commercial center throughout history.

8

Home to Many Scholars

Hama has been home to many famous scholars and intellectuals throughout history, including the renowned historian Ibn al-Athir and the poet Abu al-Ala al-Ma'arri, who was born nearby.
