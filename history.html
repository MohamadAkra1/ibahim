History of Hama

Over 8,000 years of civilization on the banks of the Orontes River

A Journey Through Time

Key moments in the history of one of the world's oldest cities

6000 BC - Ancient Hamath

Hama was first settled around 6000 BC, making it one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. The ancient city was known as "Hamath" and was a significant Canaanite city-state.

2300 BC - Kingdom of Hamath

The Kingdom of Hamath became a powerful Amorite state, mentioned in the Egyptian records of Pharaoh Thutmose III. The city was a major political and commercial center in the ancient Near East.

720 BC - Assyrian Conquest

The Assyrian king Sargon II conquered Hamath, ending its independence. The city became an important provincial capital in the Assyrian Empire.

332 BC - Hellenistic Period

After Alexander the Great's conquest, Hama (known as Epiphania) became part of the Seleucid Empire. Greek culture and architecture influenced the city during this period.

64 BC - Roman Era

The Roman general Pompey incorporated Hama into the Roman province of Syria. The city thrived under Roman rule with impressive temples, theaters, and public buildings.

636 AD - Islamic Conquest

Arab Muslim forces under Khalid ibn al-Walid conquered Hama. The city gradually became an important center of Islamic culture and learning.

10th-12th Century - Golden Age

During the Umayyad and Abbasid periods, Hama flourished as a center of science, literature, and trade. The famous historian Ibn al-Athir was from Hama.

1108 - Crusader Period

The Crusaders besieged Hama but failed to capture it. The city remained under Muslim control and continued to prosper under the Ayyubid dynasty.

1300s - Mamluk Rule

Under Mamluk rule, Hama became an important administrative center. Many beautiful buildings and mosques were constructed during this period.

1516 - Ottoman Empire

Hama became part of the Ottoman Empire after the Battle of Marj Dabiq. The city experienced a cultural renaissance with new architectural styles and urban development.

1918 - French Mandate

After World War I, Hama came under French mandate. The city played a significant role in the Syrian national movement.

1946 - Modern Syria

Hama became part of independent Syria. The city continued to grow and develop as one of Syria's major urban centers, preserving its rich heritage while embracing modernity.
