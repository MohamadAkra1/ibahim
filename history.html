History

Over 8,000 years of civilization on the banks of the Orontes

A Journey Through Time

Key moments in the history of one of the world's oldest cities

6000 BC

Hama was first settled around 6000 BC, making it one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. The ancient city was known as "Hamath."

2300 BC

The Kingdom of Hamath became a powerful Amorite state, mentioned in the Egyptian records of Pharaoh Thutmose III.

720 BC

The Assyrian king Sargon II conquered Hamath, ending its independence. The city became an important provincial capital.

332 BC

After Alexander the Great's conquest, Hama became part of the Seleucid Empire. Greek culture influenced the city.

64 BC

The Roman general Pompey incorporated Hama into the Roman province of Syria. The city thrived with impressive temples and theaters.

636 AD

Arab Muslim forces under Khalid ibn al-Walid conquered Hama. The city became an important center of Islamic culture.

10th–12th Century

During the Umayyad and Abbasid periods, Hama flourished as a center of science, literature, and trade.

1108

The Crusaders besieged Hama but failed to capture it. The city remained under Muslim control under the Ayyubid dynasty.

1300s

Under Mamluk rule, Hama became an important administrative center with many beautiful buildings and mosques.

1516

Hama became part of the Ottoman Empire. The city experienced a cultural renaissance with new architectural styles.

1918

After World War I, Hama came under French mandate. The city played a significant role in the Syrian national movement.

1946

Hama became part of independent Syria, continuing to grow as one of Syria's major urban centers.
